# Write Simply

> [!info] 文章信息
> Paul Graham / 2 min / 2021-03 / [原文链接](https://www.paulgraham.com/simply.html)

## 核心观点

写作应追求「词隐意显」——读者把认知能量花在想法上，而非解码文字上。简洁不是偷懒，而是对读者的尊重，也是让自己诚实的方式。

## 为什么写简单

- **阅读摩擦越低，读者走得越远**：大多数读者读到一半就能量衰减，降低阅读阻力能让更多人读到最后。
- **对非母语读者更公平**：很多读者对 ideas 的理解远超对英语词汇的掌握，不能因为话题难就用难词。
- **简洁让写作经得起时间**：语言和文化会变，简单文字更耐读。这不是虚荣，就像木匠把椅子做结实一样——持久本身就是质量的标志。

## saltintesta：理想的写作状态

Graham 从意大利菜 *saltimbocca*（"跳进嘴里"）造了一个词 *saltintesta*——想法跳进你脑子里，你几乎没注意到承载它们的文字。写作不可能纯粹是想法，但大多数时候这才是目标。

## 华丽写作的问题

- 华丽不仅隐藏思想，还能**隐藏思想的缺席**——这就是为什么有人这样写
- 简洁写作让自己诚实：如果你什么都没说，一目了然
- 复杂句子和花哨词汇可以**偶尔用于效果**，但绝不能是无意识的习惯

## Graham 的写作方法

初稿快速写完，然后花几天编辑、精简、打磨。大量编辑就是删减，删减让简洁变得更简洁。

## 个人思考

- [ ] 自检：写作时是否有意使用复杂句式？是否为了「显得专业」而用难词？
- [ ] 实践：初稿写完后，专门做一轮「砍掉所有不必要修饰」的编辑

## 原文摘录

> [!note]-
> I try to write using ordinary words and simple sentences. That kind of writing is easier to read, and the easier something is to read, the more deeply readers will engage with it. The less energy they expend on your prose, the more they'll have left for your ideas. And the further they'll read.
>
> There's an Italian dish called *saltimbocca*, which means "leap into the mouth." My goal when writing might be called *saltintesta*: the ideas leap into your head and you barely notice the words that got them there.
>
> Plus it's more considerate to write simply. When you write in a fancy way to impress people, you're making them do extra work just so you can seem cool. It's like trailing a long train behind you that readers have to carry.
>
> And remember, if you're writing in English, that a lot of your readers won't be native English speakers. Their understanding of ideas may be way ahead of their understanding of English.
>
> Of course, fancy writing doesn't just conceal ideas. It can also conceal the lack of them. That's why some people write that way, to conceal the fact that they have nothing to say. Whereas writing simply keeps you honest. If you say nothing simply, it will be obvious to everyone, including you.
>
> Simple writing also lasts better. People reading your stuff in the future will be in much the same position as people from other countries reading it today. The culture and the language will have changed. It's not vain to care about that, any more than it's vain for a woodworker to build a chair to last. Indeed, lasting is not merely an accidental quality of chairs, or writing. It's a sign you did a good job.
>
> The main reason I write simply is that it offends me not to. When I write a sentence that seems too complicated, or that uses unnecessarily intellectual words, it doesn't seem fancy to me. It seems clumsy.
>
> I write the first draft fast, then spend days editing it, trying to get everything just right. Much of this editing is cutting, and that makes simple writing even simpler.
